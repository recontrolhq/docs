A space is a completely isolated financial context. Every account, transaction, and balance in Recontrol belongs to exactly one space. Nothing leaks between spaces.

## Why isolation matters

Without isolation, personal and shared finances bleed into each other. Recontrol solves this with hard boundaries: a member of your household space cannot see your personal accounts, and your personal transactions have no effect on the household ledger.

This is not a view filter. It is a data boundary. Accounts, transactions, roles, and memberships are all strictly scoped to the space they were created in.

## Personal vs. shared spaces

Every user gets a **Personal** space on sign-up. It is private by default — no one else can be added to it. You can create additional **Shared** spaces and invite other people to them, with full control over what each person can see and do via [Collaboration](/features/collaboration).

## Spaces as the unit of collaboration

When you share a space, you are choosing to share an entire financial context — not a single account or transaction. Everyone in the space sees the same accounts and the same real-time balances. This makes spaces the right unit for households, partnerships, and groups.

---
title: "Our Story"
description: "Recontrol is a money modelling engine built out of frustration. Manual-first, private, and designed for shared use."
---

We tried hundreds of money apps before building this one. Seriously — hundreds. Across every platform, price point, and philosophy.

Almost all of them had the same problems.

**No single product got the full picture right.** You could either track personal spending, or manage shared expenses, or see net worth, or run a budget — but rarely more than one of those in the same place, and never all of them together in a coherent model.

The **UI was bad**. Cluttered dashboards, inconsistent flows, gestures that made no sense, screens that surfaced the wrong information at the wrong time. Apps that felt like they were designed by an accountant for an accountant, not for a person who just wants to know where their money went.

The worst part was how most apps **confused the data model entirely**. An outgoing transaction was treated as an expense by default. An incoming one was income. But that is not how money actually works. Paying off a credit card is not an expense — it is a transfer from an asset to a liability. Receiving a loan is not income — it is an increase in both an asset and a liability simultaneously. Nobody was making the distinction between expenses and liabilities, or between income and assets. The books looked fine until they didn't, and you could never quite trust the numbers.

Recontrol exists because we got fed up with that.

It uses double-entry bookkeeping — the same method banks and accountants have used for centuries — so every transaction is recorded correctly by construction. An expense is a spending category. A liability is a debt. They are not the same thing, and the app never pretends they are.

It does a small number of things. It does them well. That is the whole point.
